Choices in more depth

What choices are available when adopting Autonomous Vehicles?
There are a few different main areas of Automation that have different options available to them.

The first is how far will we go with automation?
Right now, the Society for Automotive Engineers has broken down the degree of how autonomous a vehicle is, to 6 different levels, from fully driver controlled, to fully automated driving.

As technology grows, we will slowly progress towards level 6, though it is unsure just where precisely we will end up.
Currently, we already have multiple levels in use, with Tesla’s cars already able to drive at level three, but we still see the vast majority of vehicles at level one.

A second major area of choice is in the technology used.

The most vital part of technology involved in self-driving cars are the sensors which allow the car to see its surroundings.
Currently, different developers use different sensors in combination with each other -
  • Cameras
  • Radar
  • LiDAR
  • Ultrasonic wheel sensors

Additionally, there are choices to be made about how to present this data to the driver, as well as how to present the data to other vehicles. Possible interfaces include a heads-up display, or different screens.
When communicating with other cars, a variety of different methods can be used, from cellular data to radio signals.
But finally, the most important area of things that society will need to make a choice about is how to legislate Autonomous Vehicles
The technology used for autonomous vehicles is heavily reliant upon machine learning algorithms, however, there currently isn’t a single standard across the industry on how machine learning is trained, tested, or validated.
In fact, there isn’t a single standard across the industry for anything concerning autonomous vehicles.
As we move forward with this technology, governments will have to decide how to legislate vehicles going forward, setting a unified standard for such things as:

  • Machine learning
  • Sensors required
  • Specific functions such as automated lane keeping systems
  • Operational learning
  • Right of way and priority of life when faced with unavoidable crashes.
Currently there is a voluntary international standard for some of these, however there are no specific regulations regarding these important functions.
Though governments currently have limited legislation in place just to allow testing, as the technology progresses, regulations will have to progress with it.
